The Evolution of Bicycle

In 1817 Baron von Drais invented a walking machine that would help him get arund the royal gardens faster: two same-size in-line wheels, the front one steerable, mounted in a frame upon which you straddled. The device was propelled by pushing your feet against the ground, thus rolling yourself and the device forward in a sort of gliding walk.

The mechine became known as the Draisienne (or"hobby horse"). It was made entirely of wood. This enjoyed a short lived popularity as a fad. not being practical for transportation in any other place than a well maintained pathway such a in a park or garden.

The next appearance of a two-wheeled riding mechine was in 1865, when pedals were applied directly to the front wheel. This mechine was known as the velocipede (meaning "fast foot") as well as the "boon shaker", since it's wooden structure combined with the cobblestone roads of the day made for and indoor riding academies, similar to roller rinks, could be found in large cities.
